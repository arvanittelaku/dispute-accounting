FAQs:
Q: What dispute accounting 
issues arise in financial services?
A: Financial services disputes 
requiring accounting expert 
evidence include: investment 
management mandate breaches 
(comparing actual portfolio 
performance to the mandated 
benchmark); ISDA close-out 
amount disputes (assessing 
commercial reasonableness); 
and regulatory enforcement 
proceedings where accounting 
evidence on financial position 
or benefit from misconduct 
is required.
Q: What is a "close-out amount" 
dispute in derivatives?
A: When an ISDA master agreement 
is terminated early (e.g. 
following a default), the 
non-defaulting party calculates 
a "close-out amount" representing 
the value of terminated 
transactions. Disputes arise 
when the defaulting party 
challenges this calculation. 
Dispute accountants assess 
whether the methodology 
was commercially reasonable 
and consistent with 
the ISDA definitions.

construction-engineering:
H1: Construction & Engineering 
    Dispute Accounting Expert UK
Key issues: loss and expense; 
prolongation costs; 
disruption; quantum schedules; 
JCT/NEC/FIDIC; TCC.
FAQs:
Q: Do construction disputes 
need an accountant or a 
quantity surveyor?
A: Construction disputes 
often need both. A quantity 
surveyor (QS) addresses 
the entitlement under 
the contract and the 
valuation of physical works. 
A dispute accountant 
addresses the financial 
accounting impact — 
overhead recovery, 
loss of profit, 
the treatment of costs 
in the company accounts, 
and whether costs claimed 
are properly supported 
by financial records.
Q: How does a dispute accountant 
support TCC proceedings?
A: In Technology and Construction 
Court proceedings, dispute 
accountants provide CPR Part 35 
expert reports on financial 
aspects of the claim — 
loss of profit on the 
contract, head office 
overhead recovery (Emden 
or Hudson formula), 
interest calculations, 
and any accounting issues 
in the financial accounts.
